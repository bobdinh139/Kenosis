Biography

    

Malala Yousafzai was born on July 12, 1997 in Mingora, Pakinstan. She is the youngest person to win the Nobel Prize (age 17 in 2014) after surviving the Taliban's murder attempt. She is an advocate for girls' education, which resulted in her death threat.
October 9, 2012, Malala was shot when she was traveling home from school, but she survived. In 2013, she published her first book, I am Malala and gave a speech to the United Nations.

Stories

    

Girls' education

Baby girls were not always welcome in Pakistan, but her father was determined to give her the same opportunity as a boy. As a result, she found the importance of equality. Everything were fine, but it changed a lot when the Taliban took control. THey said girl cannot go to school. In january 2008, Malala (11 years old) decided to say goodbye to her classmate. Four years later, she spoke publicly about this topic knowing that this might get her killed. From the young age, she already demonstrates kenosis. She tried to fight for not just herself, but for every girls.

Got Shot

Because she was a advocate for girl's education, the Taliban wanted to shut her mouth and attempted to assasin her. In October, a masked gunman shot on the left side of her head. But that did not stop her from doing what she wanted and what is right. Even though she already got shot and it almost killed her, she still kept fighting for everybody and girls' rights as well as women's rights.

Keep Fighting

10 days later, she woke up in a hospital in Birmingham, England. People around the world were praying for her. The words were heard. She sacrified herself for girls' education rights and in the bigger picture, for women's rights.
SHe continued to fight until every girl could go to school. She and her father established Malala fund, a charity dedicated to giving girl equal opportunity.
Because of her determination to fight, whatever it costs, for girls' education peacefully, she received the Nobel Peace Prize in December 2014 -- the youngest to win a Nobel prize. At this point, her kenosis was rewarded.

Reflection

This can influence many girls that are too scared to speak up for themselves. If there is a will, there is a way. Therefore, if I found anything that is unfair, I will not scare to speak up. This not only helps me, but also helps pther people who have similar problems. I will try to fight for what is right even though it may cost everything. I will try to give other people hope by doing good things like this.
